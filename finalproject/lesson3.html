Writing Powerful Work Experience

The Work Experience section is where you prove your value. Employers don’t just want to know what you did — they want to know how well you did it.

Key Principles:

  • Start each bullet point with a strong action verb.
  • Focus on achievements, not duties.
  • Use numbers or measurable results wherever possible.
  • Keep your formatting clean: Job Title | Company | Dates
❌ Weak Example:
- Responsible for managing social media pages.

✅ Strong Example:
- Increased social media engagement by 45% through consistent content strategy and analytics tracking.

Powerful Action Verbs:

Use words like: Led, Improved, Designed, Increased, Managed, Coordinated, Developed, Streamlined, Achieved, Delivered.

Format Example:
Digital Marketing Specialist | ABC Agency | Accra, Ghana | Jan 2020 – Present
- Managed a team of 3 to deliver 10+ successful campaigns monthly.
- Improved conversion rates by 30% through A/B testing and copy refinement.
