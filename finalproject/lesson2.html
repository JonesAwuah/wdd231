Lesson 2: Contact Information & Summary

Learn how to make a great first impression by presenting your contact details and professional summary effectively.

Progress: Lesson 2 of 8 (25%)

Crafting a Strong Introduction

Your contact information and professional summary are the first things employers see on your resume. A clean, accurate presentation ensures they can reach you easily and get an instant sense of your professional value.

Contact Information Essentials:

  • Include your full name, phone number, professional email, and location (city, country).
  • Include links to your LinkedIn profile or portfolio website if relevant.
  • Avoid using unprofessional email addresses (e.g., “cooldude@gmail.com”).
✅ Example:
Mary Johnson | Accra, Ghana | (233) 555-4321 | mary.johnson@gmail.com | linkedin.com/in/maryjohnson

Writing a Compelling Summary:

Your summary should highlight your top skills, experience level, and what makes you unique. Keep it concise — about 3–4 sentences that capture your strengths and career goals.

❌ Weak Example:
“I am looking for a job that helps me learn new things.”

✅ Strong Example:
“Results-driven administrative professional with 4+ years of experience supporting office operations and improving efficiency. Skilled in organization, scheduling, and communication, with a strong record of reliability and team collaboration.”
